## 2. Findings Catalog

Severity scale: **C** (Critical, blocks Phase 1) · **H** (High, blocks mainnet) · **M** (Medium, blocks 2-city pilot) · **L** (Low) · **I** (Info).

### ADV-F-001 — USDC freeze authority is a single-point centralization risk · **H**

**Claim**: USDC-denominated escrow and collateral give Circle (USDC issuer) the ability to freeze any BlockAssist PDA. A regulatory action or court order against Circle, or against BlockAssist itself, can immediately render escrow funds unspendable.

**Evidence**: USDC retains `freezeAuthority` on its mint. Tornado Cash sanction (2022) and subsequent OFAC-driven Circle freezes are precedent.

**Recommendation**:
- Document this risk in `CLAUDE.md` and `docs/legal/COMPLIANCE_GATE.md` (added in COMPLIANCE-001 task).
- Evaluate dual-stable strategy (USDC + PYUSD or USDC + DAI/USDS on Solana) for collateral; keep escrow USDC-only if user-facing UX requires it.
- Add `escrow_frozen` state to the escrow program design so the protocol degrades gracefully rather than reverting.

**Owner**: Lead Smart Contract Engineer + Legal Lead

---

### ADV-F-002 — Upgrade authority for Anchor programs is unspecified · **C**

**Claim**: A program with a team-controlled upgrade authority is functionally a custodial product. v2.1 documents do not specify whether programs will be: (a) team-multisig upgradeable, (b) DAO upgradeable, (c) immutable post-audit. Each choice has very different regulatory and trust implications.

**Recommendation**:
- Decide upgrade-authority model **before any contract implementation begins**. Default proposal: team multisig (≥3-of-5) for first 6 months post-mainnet, with public timelock (≥48h) and on-chain announcement. Migrate to DAO control gated on token-launch decision.
- Log decision in `DECISIONS.md`.

**Owner**: Lead Smart Contract Engineer + PM

---

### ADV-F-003 — "Optimistic release" mechanism is named but not designed · **H**

**Claim**: `README.md:8` references "USDC escrow with optimistic release". No design document exists. Key parameters undefined: challenge window length, who must affirmatively act, fallback if user is unreachable, defense against assistant-spam to game silent acceptance, MEV/front-run exposure on release.

**Recommendation**:
- Draft `docs/architecture/escrow-state-machine.md` before Phase 1 with: states, transitions, timeouts, actor incentives, and adversarial scenarios (user disappears, assistant disappears, dispute mid-release).
- Specifically address: how does the system avoid being a money transmitter under FinCEN rules? Optimistic release with a hold period is a custody pattern.

**Owner**: Lead Smart Contract Engineer

---

### ADV-F-004 — On-chain jury implementation: no existence proof on Solana · **H**

**Claim**: v2.1 mandates "Solana-native optimistic + on-chain jury fallback" as the dispute primary, with Kleros demoted to experimental. The decision is sound in principle (avoids bridge risk), but no production-grade on-chain jury implementation on Solana is cited.

**Evidence**: Kleros itself shipped on EVM. Reality.eth and UMA are EVM. Solana-native equivalents (e.g., Realms voting, Squads multisig) are not jury systems.

**Recommendation**:
- Conduct landscape review and document in `docs/architecture/dispute-system.md`. Acceptable outcomes: (a) cite an existing Solana primitive we will reuse, (b) commit to building one with explicit scope and audit budget, (c) revisit the "Kleros experimental" decision.
- Until landscape review is done, do not commit to "Solana-native primary" in marketing or contracts.

**Owner**: Lead Smart Contract Engineer + Security Lead

### ADV-F-005 — Slashing parameters fully undefined · **C**

**Claim**: Collateral exists in name only. Missing: slashing trigger conditions, decision authority (auto vs. jury), magnitude (% or absolute), destination (insurance pool / user / burn / ops), appeal path, statute of limitations.

**Recommendation**:
- Halt all collateral program work until `docs/architecture/slashing-spec.md` is drafted and reviewed by Legal Lead. Slashing assistant funds without due process is exactly the pattern that triggers consumer-protection enforcement.

**Owner**: Lead Smart Contract Engineer + Legal Lead + Tokenomics Engineer

### ADV-F-006 — Fee-funded $ASSIST staker yield is securities-shaped · **C**

**Claim**: `docs/tokenomics/model.md` allocates 3.0% of the 6% fee to "Ecosystem / Staker Yield (30-40% of fees to stakers)". A token whose holders receive a passive yield generated by the work of others (assistants on the platform) is the textbook fact pattern in *SEC v. Howey* and *Reves v. Ernst & Young*. This is not just a marketing risk — it is enforcement risk for the issuer.

**Evidence**: SEC v. LBRY, SEC v. Coinbase (staking-as-a-service line), and the March 2026 staff guidance referenced in `COMPLIANCE_GATE.md`.

**Recommendation**:
- **HALT** any token launch action until securities counsel issues a written opinion (LEGAL-002).
- Until then, treat $ASSIST as if it will be deemed a security and design conservative offering structure (Reg D 506(c) or Reg S, non-US accredited only, lockups).
- Accelerate the No-Token Alternative track so we have a viable non-token launch path if counsel says "no".

**Owner**: Legal Lead + Tokenomics Engineer + PM

---

### ADV-F-007 — Insurance backstop has no entity, no policy, no funding model · **C**

**Claim**: `docs/tokenomics/model.md` allocates 1.8% of fees to "Insurance Pool". `COMPLIANCE_GATE.md` mentions "Insurance backstop legal structure" as a gating item. There is no specification for: legal entity holding the pool, who can draw from it, payout policy, reinsurance, or what happens when a loss event exceeds the pool.

**Recommendation**:
- Halt insurance-pool implementation until a written design exists.
- Investigate Nexus Mutual–style protocol cover vs. a captive insurer vs. a Bermuda/Cayman vehicle. Each has very different cost, latency, and regulatory profile.
- Per-user payout cap is essential; without it, a single large-order failure drains the pool.

**Owner**: Legal Lead + Tokenomics Engineer

---

### ADV-F-008 — Gig worker classification likely fails CA ABC test as designed · **H**

**Claim**: California's AB5 ABC test, and analogues (NY, MA, NJ, EU platform-work directive), classify a worker as an employee unless the platform proves the worker is (A) free from control, (B) performing work outside the usual course of the platform's business, (C) customarily engaged in an independent trade. A platform that sets pricing, manages disputes, holds collateral, and operates the marketplace likely fails (A) and (B).

**Decentralization defense**: a court has not yet accepted "the protocol is decentralized" as severing the employer relationship. The party that ultimately controls the multisig is typically the employer-of-record.

**Recommendation**:
- Legal Lead must scope this **before** city selection. Pilot cities outside CA/MA/NJ for the first 12 months. Document in `COMPLIANCE_GATE.md`.
- Avoid product features that increase control (price-setting, mandatory acceptance, scheduling). Push these to assistants where possible.

**Owner**: Legal Lead + PM

---

### ADV-F-009 — No identity primitive specified; reputation system is Sybil-vulnerable · **H**

**Claim**: Reputation-weighted yield (`docs/tokenomics/model.md`) and any jury-based dispute resolution both presuppose Sybil resistance. v2.1 names neither a privacy-preserving identity primitive (e.g., World ID, Civic, Polygon ID, BrightID) nor a Solana-native equivalent. Reputation built purely from on-chain order history is gameable by self-dealing: an assistant transacts with their own funded sock-puppet to farm reputation.

**Recommendation**:
- Add `docs/architecture/identity-and-sybil.md`. Acceptable bars: proof-of-personhood at sign-up OR economic-cost Sybil resistance (collateral threshold high enough that farming N identities costs more than the reputation is worth).
- Until specified, do not ship reputation-weighted yield.

**Owner**: Security Lead + Lead Smart Contract Engineer

---

### ADV-F-010 — Money transmitter licensing scope likely under-estimated · **H**

**Claim**: `COMPLIANCE_GATE.md` mentions MTL assessment. Typical industry experience: MTL acquisition is $1–5M per state in capital + bonding + legal, and 12–24 months elapsed. Operating in 2 US pilot cities without MTL is plausible only if escrow structure qualifies for an exemption (agent-of-payee, payment processor, etc.), which depends on contract terms.

**Recommendation**:
- Legal Lead provides written analysis on MTL applicability **before** city selection. If MTLs are required, the 12–18 month timeline is unrealistic.
- Consider non-US pilot cities (e.g., Lisbon, Buenos Aires, Singapore) where licensing path is clearer.

**Owner**: Legal Lead + PM

## 3. Decision Challenges

Each row in `docs/status/PROJECT_STATUS.md:Key Decisions Log` is steel-manned then attacked.

### "Remove 'locked / no rework' policy" — **AGREE**
The original policy created a deception risk: pressure to ignore new information. Removing it is correct. **Counter-argument considered**: without "locked" status, scope creeps. **Response**: scope discipline lives in sprint planning and `DECISIONS.md`, not in a brittle invariant. Keep removal.

### "Add Legal & Compliance Lead + hard Compliance Gate" — **AGREE**
Existential risk. **Counter-argument**: gate may delay shipping indefinitely if Legal is risk-averse. **Response**: the gate is a *checklist*, not subjective approval. Each item is binary. If Legal blocks beyond the checklist, that's a separate escalation. Accept.

### "Change collateral to USDC-denominated" — **AGREE WITH CAVEAT**
Volatile-collateral protocols are a known anti-pattern (see MakerDAO Black Thursday). USDC is the right default. **Caveat**: USDC freeze risk (ADV-F-001) must be documented. Dual-stable for collateral is worth evaluating.

### "Drop Pinocchio as Phase 0 mandate" — **AGREE STRONGLY**
Anchor is the safer, faster path to MVP. Pinocchio is a micro-optimization. Don't pay micro-optimization complexity costs before measuring.

### "Drop Alpenglow as locked requirement" — **AGREE STRONGLY**
Building against unfinished consensus features is bug-chasing. Revisit when the feature ships.

### "Prioritize Solana-native dispute fallback" — **CONDITIONAL AGREE**
Avoiding bridge risk is correct. **But**: this trades known cross-chain risk for unknown on-chain-jury-on-Solana risk (ADV-F-004). Validate the implementation path before treating this as settled.

### "Add Adversarial Reviewer role with halt authority" — **AGREE**
The role must be filled by a named human, not only by an AI (ADV-F-013).
